About the Game

osu! is a popular freeware rhythm game created by Australian developer Dean Herbert, also known as "peppy." It was initially released on September 16, 2007, for Microsoft Windows, and later expanded to macOS, Linux, Android, and iOS. The gameplay of osu! is inspired by the Nintendo DS rhythm game Osu! Tatakae! Ouendan and its sequel Elite Beat Agents. Players click on notes, which appear as circles, using the mouse cursor. The objective is to click on the circles at the appropriate time and in the correct order, aided by rings called approach circles that close in on the hit circles to visually indicate the timing

Osu! System Requirements

PC Requirements

Minimum:

  • OS: Windows 7 or higher (64-bit)
  • Processor: 1 GHz+ Processor
  • Memory: 512 MB+ RAM
  • Graphics: Any DirectX 9 compatible GPU
  • DirectX: Version 9.0c
  • Storage: 2 GB available space

Recommended:

  • OS: Windows 10
  • Processor: 2 GHz+ Processor
  • Memory: 1 GB+ RAM
  • Graphics: Any DirectX 9 compatible GPU
  • DirectX: 2 GB available space

Console Availability

  • PlayStation 4
  • PlayStation 5
  • PlayStation VR
  • PlayStation VR2
