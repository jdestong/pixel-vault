About the Game

Dance Dance Revolution (DDR) is a pioneering rhythm and dance video game series developed by Konami. It was first introduced in Japan in 1998 as part of the Bemani series and later released in North America and Europe in 1999. In DDR, players stand on a dance platform or stage and hit colored arrows laid out in a cross with their feet to musical and visual cues. The objective is to step on the arrows in time with the music, matching the patterns presented on the screen. Players are judged based on their accuracy and timing, and they can choose more songs to play if they receive a passing score.
